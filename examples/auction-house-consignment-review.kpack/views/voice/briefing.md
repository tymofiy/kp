Here is a briefing on the consignment under review.

The work is a thirty-eight-centimetre bronze figure attributed by the consignor to Sculptor Y. The reserve was set at one hundred and eighty thousand euro.

(pause)

The recommendation is to decline the consignment as submitted.

The decision rests on the foundry mark. The underside carries the mark of Foundry F in Paris. Foundry F began operating in nineteen twenty-five, twelve years after Sculptor Y's death. Any cast bearing this mark is therefore a posthumous cast, regardless of how faithfully the model is rendered.

(pause)

The 1971 catalog provided by the consignor is genuine and not in error. It describes the work as by Sculptor Y, which was the convention of its time. Conventions distinguishing lifetime from posthumous casts strengthened in the nineteen eighties, after that catalog was published.

The reserve appears calibrated to the lifetime-cast market range, which is between three hundred and twenty thousand and five hundred and forty thousand euro. The posthumous-cast range, in contrast, is between eighty thousand and one hundred and forty thousand euro.

(pause)

The consignment can be reconsidered on revised terms. If the consignor agrees to a posthumous-cast attribution and a reserve aligned with that market range, we can include the work in the sale.

This is not a verdict on the work. It is a decision about whether this house can accept this consignment under the attribution as submitted.
