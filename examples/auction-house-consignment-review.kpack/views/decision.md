# Consignment Review — Committee Decision

## Recommendation

**Decline the consignment as submitted.** The recommendation is open to reconsideration on revised terms (see Conditions).

## What is established

- The work is a 38-cm bronze figure with the sculptor's signature and a foundry mark from Foundry F, Paris.
- Foundry F began operating in 1925 — twelve years after Sculptor Y's death. Any cast bearing the F mark is therefore posthumous, regardless of model fidelity.
- The 1971 estate-sale catalog cited by the consignor is genuine but predates the conventions that distinguish lifetime from posthumous casts. The catalog is not wrong; it does not establish what the consignor claims it establishes.
- The reserve of €180,000 is positioned between the comparable lifetime-cast range (€320,000–€540,000) and the posthumous-cast range (€80,000–€140,000), suggesting calibration to the lifetime-cast attribution.

## Why decline rather than re-list

Two paths were considered and rejected:

1. **List "by Sculptor Y" as submitted.** Rejected: exposes the house to reputational and rescission risk if a buyer subsequently identifies the foundry-mark evidence. Comparable mis-cataloguing has been the basis of post-sale claims in the field.
2. **Reattribute in catalog text without consignor agreement.** Rejected: the house's standard consignment agreement reserves attribution-text rights to the house but requires consignor sign-off on attribution-language changes. Unilateral reattribution would breach the agreement.

The remaining path — decline pending consignor agreement to a revised attribution — is the recommendation.

## Conditions for reconsideration

The consignment may be reconsidered if:

- The consignor agrees to a catalog attribution of "after Sculptor Y, posthumous cast, Foundry F, c. 1925–1940."
- The reserve is revised to align with the posthumous-cast market range (€80,000–€140,000).
- Both terms are reflected in a revised consignment agreement.

## What does not change

- The work itself is well-cast, in good condition, and consistent with Foundry F's quality during the relevant period. Posthumous casts after Sculptor Y are saleable; the issue is the attribution and reserve, not the work.
- The 1971 catalog and the family provenance chain remain valid evidence of the work's history; they do not establish lifetime authorship and do not need to.
