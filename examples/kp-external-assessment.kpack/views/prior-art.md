# Prior Art

> Genealogy, adjacent systems, and the sharpest comparisons from the prior art
> report.

## Genealogy

The prior art report frames KP:1 as a convergence of six traditions:

- Semantic Web thinking about structured claims and provenance
- compact argumentation syntax
- nanopublications and micropublications
- ADR-style lifecycle and supersession
- literate programming's union of reasoning and artifact
- Markdown optimized for AI consumption

The report's point is not that KP:1 is detached from prior work. It is that the
combination is unusual and worth taking seriously as a synthesis.

## Closest Adjacent Systems

The nearest comparisons are distributed across different layers:

- Semantic Web standards provide stronger interoperability and formal semantics
- knowledge graphs provide stronger identity and large-scale querying
- PKM tools provide friendlier authoring but weaker epistemic structure
- RAG and document pipelines provide easier ingestion but weaker authorship of
  belief state
- argumentation systems provide cleaner formal dispute models
- preservation standards provide better integrity guarantees

This view is useful because it prevents a false binary. KP:1 does not need to
beat every system on every axis. It needs to be clear about which layer it is
trying to own.

## Where KP:1 Looks Most Distinct

The prior art report highlights five differentiators:

- confidence and investigation depth as separate axes
- typed contradiction
- three-surface rendering
- deterministic definition/policy types beside probabilistic claims
- lifecycle with consolidation

Those are also the features that are hardest to borrow from a single existing
standard.

## Where Prior Art Still Wins

The gaps are straightforward:

- interoperability and global identity
- formal reasoning
- large-scale retrieval and automation
- collaborative authoring and consensus processes
- byte-level integrity and preservation machinery

These are not arguments against KP:1's existence. They are the map of where a
future bridge, export layer, or core-spec reduction would matter most.

## The Open Question

The prior art report is crisp about the question that remains:

Does author-curated, pre-structured epistemic state outperform inference-time
structurization for the tasks KP:1 is designed to support?

That question is not resolved by genealogy alone. It requires benchmarking.
