# Solar Energy Market: An Analytical Assessment

This assessment covers utility-scale solar market trends, cost trajectories, and regional adoption patterns, current as of 18 March 2026. Confidence appears as explicit probabilities (Sherman Kent–style, normalized); each finding names its kind — observation, report, computation, or inference — plus its depth, date, and sources.

## Thesis

Our central thesis: the decline in solar costs is structural, not cyclical — driven by manufacturing scale. An inference and a judgment held at roughly 95% after exhaustive investigation (1 March 2026), grounded in our internal multi-source synthesis and IRENA's *Renewable Power Generation Costs 2025*. All ten synthesized analyses converged on it; the learning curve — a 22% cost reduction per capacity doubling — has held for forty years. This thesis and the grid-parity finding below support one another.

Grid parity is already achieved in 75% of global markets — an investigated inference and judgment at roughly 90% (1 March 2026), grounded in the multi-source synthesis (the IRENA/BloombergNEF data). Subsidy removal in mature markets has not reversed adoption. It supports the structural thesis.

A minority view stands against the thesis: a cost floor is approaching, and further declines will slow significantly. A reported claim, investigated, at low confidence — roughly 30% (14 February 2026) — from the MIT Energy Initiative's *The Future of Solar Energy* (updated 2025). Silicon material costs impose a theoretical floor; three of ten analyses supported this framing. Its disagreement with the structural-decline thesis is a productive tension, not an error: both rest on credible evidence, and the tension itself is informative — it reveals the timeline question.

## Technology

Bifacial modules are now 70% of new installations globally — a direct observation at roughly 99% (18 March 2026), per the IEA PVPS *Trends Report 2026*. Rear-side gain runs 5–25% depending on albedo; bankability is established; monofacial is declining to niche applications.

Perovskite tandem cells have reached 33.7% lab efficiency at Oxford PV — a direct observation at roughly 99% (18 March 2026), per Oxford PV's certified-record press release. The lab-to-fab gap remains; commercial viability at utility scale is estimated for 2028–2030. It bears on the durability finding below.

Durability is the unsolved risk for perovskite commercialization — an inference at roughly 75% (10 March 2026), resting on the Nature Energy stability review and the NREL stability assessment, dependent on the efficiency finding above. Moisture and heat degradation pathways are known but not fully mitigated; the 25-year warranty requirement is the hard gate.

## Market

Utility-scale solar is now the cheapest new electricity source globally — an investigated, direct observation at roughly 92% (1 March 2026), from the multi-source synthesis and the IRENA dataset. LCOE is below gas and wind in most markets; subsidy-independent growth is confirmed. It supports both the structural thesis and the grid-parity finding.

## Risks

Supply chain concentration in China is the highest systemic risk — an inference at roughly 90% after exhaustive investigation (1 March 2026) — a meta-level judgment about the market as a whole, not any one technology, presupposing the cost-leadership finding above. Grounded in the multi-source synthesis and BloombergNEF's *Solar Supply Chain Vulnerability Index 2026*: China produces 80% of polysilicon and 95% of wafers, and tariff or geopolitical disruption could create 12–18 month supply gaps. Eight of the ten analyses flagged this risk. It is not a technology problem — it is a trade-policy and diversification problem.

## Sources and basis

**Internal analysis — 10 independent assessments across multiple AI models** (multi-source synthesis; captured 1 March 2026). Eight distinct models across the ten analyses; all converged on cost decline being structural (manufacturing learning curve) rather than cyclical (subsidy-driven).

**IRENA — *Renewable Power Generation Costs 2025*** (research; captured 1 March 2026). Comprehensive dataset on utility-scale solar PV LCOE trends across 40 countries. Learning rate of 22% per capacity doubling confirmed across 1976–2025 data.

**MIT Energy Initiative — *The Future of Solar Energy* (updated 2025)** (research; captured 14 February 2026). Analysis of silicon cost floors and theoretical efficiency limits. Argues polysilicon pricing has limited room for further reduction, implying LCOE declines will increasingly depend on balance-of-system and soft costs.

**IEA Photovoltaic Power Systems Programme — *Trends Report 2026*** (observation; captured 18 March 2026). Global installed PV capacity figures and market share by technology type. Bifacial module share crossed 70% in Q4 2025.

**Oxford PV press release — certified tandem efficiency record** (observation; captured 18 March 2026). 33.7% certified efficiency for a perovskite–silicon tandem cell, certified by Fraunhofer ISE CalLab. Commercial module target: 2028.

**Nature Energy — "Stability challenges for perovskite photovoltaics" (2025)** (research; captured 10 March 2026). Review of degradation mechanisms: moisture ingress, thermal cycling, UV exposure. Encapsulation advances extend lab lifetime to 5,000+ hours, but field validation at 25-year scale is absent.

**NREL — *Perovskite Stability Assessment 2025*** (research; captured 10 March 2026). Accelerated aging tests on twelve encapsulation methods. Best-performing samples retained over 90% efficiency at a simulated 10-year equivalent. The gap to the 25-year warranty requirement remains the commercialization gate.

**BloombergNEF — *Solar Supply Chain Vulnerability Index 2026*** (research; captured 1 March 2026). Quantitative assessment of geographic concentration in polysilicon, wafer, cell, and module production. China's share exceeds 80% at every stage except module assembly (65%). No diversification trend visible in 2025 data.
